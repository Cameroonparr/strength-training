Squat

Traditional Squat

Muscles worked

The barbell squat is a fundamental strength training exercise that should form the cornerstone of any lower-body routine. This compound movement primarily targets the quadriceps, glutes, and hamstrings while also engaging the core, hip flexors, and strengthening various tendons and ligaments in the lower body. Incorporating barbell squats can significantly improve overall stability, strength, and mobility.

Starting position

To begin, place your hands roughly shoulder width apart and grip the bar. Ensure that the bar is just below your neck.

Lifting the bar

Gently lift the bar off of the rack and take a step backwards. Ensure that your feet are like your hands, shoulder width apart.

Lowering the bar

Engage your core and slowly lower yourself until your thighs are at least parallel to the floor. Keep your knees aligned behind your toes by shifting your hips back as you descend.

Repeat!

Now, repeat for as many repetitions as you’re comfortable with, ensuring each one is controlled and steady.
