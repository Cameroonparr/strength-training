Benchpress

Barbell Benchpress

Muscles worked

The Barbell Bench Press is one of the most widely recognized compound weightlifting movements. Its excellent for building upper body strength as it stimulates multiple muscles, most notably the pectoralis major. While many visualize a larger chest, it also enhances shoulder and arm size by working the anterior deltoids (front shoulder) and triceps brachii (antagonist to the biceps).

Starting position

To begin, place your hands roughly shoulder width apart and grip the bar. Ensure that the bar is inline with your chest.

Lifting the bar

Keep your core tight and your shoulders retracted for stability. In a slow and controlled manner, push the bar upwards.

Lowering the bar

Gently lower the bar to your chest. This is known as concentric movement, where the muscle exerts force while shortening. This controlled motion provides maximum stimulation and promotes muscle growth. It is also important to avoid bouncing the bar off of your chest when performing a high number of repititions. You are only cheating yourself if you do!

Repeat!

Now repeat for as many repitions as you wish! Remember: SLOW and STEADY movements to help activate your muscle and avoid injury.
